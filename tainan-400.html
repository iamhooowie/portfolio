Tainan 400 - Education for All
Role: Lead Product Designer # Game Planning # User Experience # Graphic Design # User Interface # Coding

Tainan, the historical cradle of Taiwan, has meticulously preserved its myriad of historic landmarks. The year 2024 marked the 400th anniversary of this venerable city. The Tainan City Government hosted a variety of commemorative activities across sectors, including sports, agriculture, healthcare, etc. A key component of these commemorations was the Tainan 400 Public Education Initiative, designed to deepen public understanding of the evolution of educational knowledge.

Goal

Featuring GPS and motion recognition features, these games are set in the historic Tainan Park, utilizing distinctive landmarks within the park as gameplay elements. The games involve puzzles and quizzes to educate players about Taiwan’s educational history. The UI is designed in a childlike, game-like style that allows players to learn about the history of education by playing games. The game is designed with interesting characters to attract young participants.

Target

  • Elementary & middle school students: primary learners experiencing history and culture
  • Teenagers: motivated by interactive challenges and social gameplay elements.
  • Parents & families: join as companions, ensuring broader community participation and engagement.

Walking in Mathematics World

During the Dutch rule period (1624-1662), Dutch missionaries in Taiwan used the Roman alphabet to compile a dictionary of the aboriginal language for evangelism and assist in administrative affairs. They used the Roman alphabet to teach the local people to write their language, which was the Sinkang language. The Sinkang language was later used primarily in contract documents called “Sinkang Manuscripts.” Although Sinkang adopted the Latin alphabet and Arabic numerals, it did not adopt a numeral correspondence system. For example, in Sinkang Manuscripts, “365” is written as 300605 (300-60-5). The level’s primary purpose is to let players understand the application of Sinkang Manuscripts at that time.

Yi Dance of Tales

Yi Dance is a kind of group dance that combines the traditional arts of ritual, music, and dance. Players will experience the wonders of Row Dance and learn the movements that symbolize honoring the gods, praying for blessings, and utilizing the motion recognition features and program to determine the correct rate of the player’s action.
